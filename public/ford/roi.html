Overview / ROI

Return on investment — the case for visibility

Kentucky Truck Plant produces high-demand, high-margin vehicles. Every minute of unplanned downtime, every quality escape, and every delayed Help/Call response has a measurable cost. The numbers below are illustrative — built from publicly available Ford production data and industry benchmarks. The functional specification engagement will replace these estimates with figures grounded in KTP's actual output, downtime history, and quality data.

The opportunity

What each OEE dimension is worth at KTP

Working assumptions (illustrative). KTP produces approximately 1,000–1,100 vehicles per day across multiple shifts. Average Super Duty transaction price ~$65,000–$75,000. Typical truck plant unplanned downtime runs 8–12% of available time. These assumptions drive the estimates below — actual figures will differ and will be refined during the data mapping workstream.
Availability — downtime reduction

~$2.1M / shift-point

Each percentage point of uptime recovered at KTP's scale and vehicle price point represents significant throughput. At 1,050 vehicles/day and ~$70K ASP, one point of availability is roughly 10–11 vehicles — approximately $700K–$770K per shift in recovered output.

Basis: 1,050 vehicles/day · $70K ASP · 1pt availability = ~10.5 vehicles/shift
Performance — JPH improvement

~$1.4M / JPH point

Closing even a portion of the gap between actual and target JPH compounds across shifts. One additional JPH sustained across a full shift adds ~20 vehicles. At KTP's vehicle mix, that is roughly $1.4M in additional output per shift.

Basis: 1 JPH × 10-hr shift × $70K ASP = ~$700K/shift per line
Quality — first-pass yield

~$800K / yield point

Each rework loop at KTP costs time on the line and carries warranty risk downstream. Closing 1 point of first-pass yield eliminates rework loops on roughly 10 vehicles per shift — and reduces the downstream warranty exposure that follows quality escapes.

Basis: 1pt FPY at 1,050 vehicles/day · rework cost + warranty tail
Help/Call — response time

~$420K / minute saved

At KTP's throughput, each minute of unplanned downtime eliminated across the plant represents roughly $420K in annualized output recovery — assuming 250 production days and current vehicle mix. Faster Help/Call response directly shortens event duration.

Basis: 1 min/event · 2 events/shift avg · 250 days · $70K ASP

The engagement cost

What the functional specification costs vs. what it enables

Functional specification engagement

Six workstreams over approximately eight weeks. Data mapping, OEE methodology definition, Help/Call workflow design, architecture recommendation, and a build-ready implementation spec.

Output: a document engineering can build from. No discovery re-work on the follow-on implementation.

What it unlocks

A follow-on implementation that is estimable, low-risk, and scoped to KTP's actual systems — not a generic deployment. The spec eliminates the most expensive part of most technology projects: discovering requirements after the build has started.

Risk reduction: a spec-first approach consistently delivers faster, lower-cost implementations.

Scalability beyond KTP

The architecture and data flow patterns developed for KTP are designed to extend to other Ford plants. The asset model, namespace structure, and integration patterns become a Ford template — not a one-off.

Multiplier: each additional plant connected costs a fraction of the first.
