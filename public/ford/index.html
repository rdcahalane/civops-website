The challenge

What is holding KTP back

Kentucky Truck Plant operates with experienced teams and high-output equipment. But the data infrastructure hasn't kept pace with the operational complexity. The result is reactive response, OEE numbers that don't reflect reality, and lost time hunting for answers that already exist — in separate systems, on separate clocks.

Problem 1

OEE numbers don't reflect reality

Reported OEE excludes quality losses and relies on buffers and manual entry. The methodology needs to be defined accurately before any improvement program can be measured.

Problem 2

Downtime is logged, not understood

Downtime events are recorded but the upstream cause is frequently lost. A stop at one station is logged at the station — not traced to the constraint that caused it.

Problem 3

Quality and throughput are siloed

Quality defects live in inspection systems. Throughput lives in the historian. No single view connects a quality trend to the asset, shift, or vehicle configuration that produced it.

Problem 4

Help/Call is informal

Downtime response depends on who notices and who calls. There is no automatic trigger, no visibility into open calls, and no data on response time by area or shift.

Problem 5

Data is trapped in separate systems

Historian, downtime system, and quality tools each hold part of the picture. Getting a complete view requires manual correlation — too slow for real-time decisions.

Problem 6

No common operating picture

Team members, supervisors, and managers see different versions of performance. The same shift looks different depending on which system you check and when you check it.

The solution

One operating picture — built on what already exists

The answer isn't replacing what works. It's adding the layer that connects the systems already in place — historian, downtime, quality inspection — and surfaces that data in real time through a common cockpit that works for every role in the plant.

Live JPH and cycle time

Actual jobs-per-hour vs. target, by line and area, updated continuously from the plant historian. No manual entry. No end-of-shift summaries.

Downtime cause — not just downtime count

Every stop attributed to a cause, an area, and a responsible party. Pareto by shift, by line, by asset — so the chronic issues surface above the noise.

Quality linked to the line

End-of-line and in-process quality defects connected to the asset, shift, operator, and vehicle configuration that produced them — turning a count into a root-cause signal.

Help/Call with automatic triggers

Stop-state detection drives automatic Help/Call initiation where possible. Open calls, response time, and resolution are visible across the area — not just to the person who made the call.

One view from floor to office

The same data, appropriately scoped — a team member sees their line, a supervisor sees their area, a manager sees the plant. No reconciliation between versions.

Built to scale

The data backbone is designed to extend to additional lines and areas without rebuilding. Connect one line well, then expand — the architecture handles it.

The deliverable

A functional specification — not a slide deck

The output of this engagement is a build-ready functional specification: data flows mapped, gaps identified, architecture defined, and a follow-on implementation scoped. Every recommendation is grounded in the actual systems and data at KTP — not a generic template.

Current-state data mapping

Document what systems exist, what data they hold, how it flows today, and where the gaps are between what's captured and what's needed for real-time OEE visibility.

OEE methodology definition

Define how cycle time, availability, and quality combine into an accurate, defensible OEE number for KTP — one that reflects operational reality and can be reported consistently.

Spec

One functional specification

All workstream outputs compiled into a single document that engineering can build from — data flows, integration points, architecture diagrams, and a follow-on implementation estimate. No discovery re-work needed.

Architecture recommendation

A data translation layer recommendation grounded in KTP's actual systems — defining the connections between historian, downtime, and quality data that make the cockpit possible.

Help/Call workflow design

A Help/Call system specification that includes automatic triggers, escalation rules, and visibility requirements — designed to work alongside the downtime system, not replace it.
