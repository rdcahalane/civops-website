Business case

Where value is expected to come from

The strongest near-term value case is not abstract AI. It is faster response, fewer repeat failures, cleaner closure data, and reduced friction between operators, maintenance, Control Room, reliability, and engineering.

Downtime recoveryReduce time lost to slow diagnosis, unclear ownership, repeated information hunting, and delayed escalation.
MTTR / response timeGive the technician likely cause, prior incidents, SOPs, parts context, and escalation support from one event record.
Repeat-failure reductionCapture reason, fix, parts, recurrence flag, and evidence so chronic issues are visible and can be addressed structurally.
Data quality for future AIUse CESMII SM Profiles + i3X-inspired data standards so analytics and AI are trained on contextualized events instead of raw system exhaust.

Ford — Dearborn assembly, 2022

Deployed predictive maintenance with structured event records across body and stamping lines. Result: 20% reduction in unplanned downtime within 14 months. Key enabler: one event record per fault with prior-incident context attached before the technician arrived.

Source: Ford Q4 2022 operations briefing / Reuters (Nov 2022)

Toyota — TNGA plant standard

World-class Toyota plants running TNGA architecture target MTTR under 3 hours and repeat-failure rate under 5%. The discipline is not the equipment — it is the structured maintenance event language and RCA close-out that creates institutional memory.

Source: Toyota Production System — Maintenance Excellence framework (public TPS documentation)

Shell — Smart Fields program

Shell's multi-year connected-operations program across upstream assets reduced unplanned downtime by up to 45% and cut maintenance cost per asset by 15–20%. The core move: contextualized event records replacing verbal and paper-based escalation.

Source: Shell Sustainability / Operations Report; McKinsey "Predictive Maintenance" (2017)
What the benchmarks say about Nissan: the outcomes are available. The gap between world-class and average performers is not equipment — it is whether maintenance events are captured, classified, and acted on from one shared record. That is exactly what this program addresses.
