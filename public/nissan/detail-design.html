Phased build

Three phases of expansion � same architecture, wider scope

Phase 1 pilots HighByte on a single asset. Each subsequent phase extends the same hub to cover more of the plant without rebuilding it.

Phase 1 scope: Read-only pilot. HighByte Intelligence Hub connects to Cimplicity, Historian, FAULT, Inspect/Autis, Robots DB, and Maximo. Humble Factory OS and Analytics/AI consume data via UNS and MCP. Output: Maintenance Cockpit for CCR � cycle time, top-3 breakdowns, uptime, Senseye PdM, and inspection QC. Fascia is out of scope; the architecture is designed to extend to it in Phase 2 without rebuilding.
Phase 2 scope: Extends the Phase 1 hub to cover Fascia front & rear � a separate engagement focused on operations data, not maintenance and asset health. New outputs: Fascia Operations Cockpit (cycle time, uptime, QC, downtime by operator/equipment). The HighByte hub is already in place; Phase 2 adds the Fascia data path, i3X templates for front/rear fascia, and activates optional write-back capability.
Phase 3 scope: Extends the same HighByte architecture across all of Paint Line 1 � a significantly larger undertaking than the Cell 56 pilot. All cells, conveyors, and support systems connect to the UNS. i3X templates expand to cover the full cell and equipment inventory. Both the CCR and Fascia Operations cockpits are fully operational. This phase validates the architecture as the plant-wide data backbone.

What works today, and the one limitation
Models will import; the instance section is best-effort. The models / attributes match HighByte's documented schema, so they import cleanly. What is not published is the exact project wrapper keys and the precise instance-binding JSON � so the per-robot binding is a best-effort mapping (attribute ? Cimplicity address) rather than a guaranteed-valid instance.

The fix is one real export. A single GET /v1/project/export from your hub � or one hand-built instance exported � lets us align the generator 1:1 with the real format. After that it auto-configures all six sealer robots, and the same pattern extends to cell 56 / base coat and any Fanuc cells straight from their Cimplicity exports.
