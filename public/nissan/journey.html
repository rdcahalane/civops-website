A more involved paint-shop day in the life

This page is built around an automotive paint-shop example so Nissan leaders can picture the target state in a specific, operationally credible setting. It combines a journey map with a storyboard, because the audience needs both the process view and the human view.

Operator
Starts with context

Knows the watch list and whether a line or cell is already at risk.

Sees early warning

Watch / inspect / intervene states make the issue visible before a full stop.

Self-serves first

Safe first action, guided troubleshooting, or call-for-help trigger.

Stays in the same case

Does not re-explain the issue after maintenance arrives.

Confirms restart

Verifies the line condition and production impact.

Hands off cleanly

Next shift sees what happened and what to watch.

Maintenance tech
Sees bad actors

Starts with priority work, open issues, and parts constraints.

Gets rich event context

Asset, symptom, and likely cause are attached automatically.

Guided diagnosis

Similar failures, PM history, manuals, and parts availability come together.

Escalates when needed

Controls / reliability / vendor support join the same thread.

Repairs and verifies

Fix, duration, and evidence stay with the original event.

Closes with standards

Reason code, corrective action, owner, and recurrence flag are captured.

Control Room / L2
Monitors risk

Sees fleet-style visibility across line, area, and chronic bad actors.

Knows when a line is drifting

Can support before the issue becomes a plant-level problem.

Receives structured escalations

No vague ticket or second-hand phone call needed.

Coordinates the response

Quality, controls, and remote expertise can be pulled in fast.

Supports verification

Confirms line, cell, and process condition after repair.

Improves playbooks

Patterns feed back into standard response logic.

Reliability / leadership
Sees the pattern

Repeat issues, overdue PMs, and asset health are visible before production starts.

Knows which issues matter

Can distinguish noise from chronic risk.

Tracks quality of response

Can see how quickly and how cleanly incidents are handled.

Learns from the event

Gets a stronger root-cause and bad-actor basis for action.

Measures throughput impact

Can connect maintenance actions to uptime and quality outcomes.

Builds the knowledge base

Every fix strengthens future performance and future AI.

Day in the life

A shift on Paint 4 — same events, different outcome

The same real events from one night shift, played two ways. The data already exists — it's just trapped in separate systems, on separate clocks, attributed to the wrong thing. The operating layer puts it in one place so the right person sees the real problem first.

05:45

Shift start — line comes up wet

Process Lead walks the line; first units enter the booth.

Today
OEE sits in the low-30s%. No one can say why in the moment — the answer is spread across Cimplicity, the FAULT app, the robot pendants, and Maximo.
With civops
The area opens on one screen — off-paint cycle time & uptime vs. target, JPH-first. The number is honest and live; the gaps are already attributed.
06:30

Spray booth starves

Topcoat 5 booth goes STARVED — the biggest downtime bucket this shift (34 min over 22 events).

Today
Logged as "Starved" — one of dozens of look-alike entries. The upstream reason (a cell not keeping pace) is lost.
With civops
STARVED shows next to the upstream cell state and conveyor block/starve signals on one timeline. The Control Room rebalances before it repeats.
08:08

A robot fault that isn't seven faults

A 24V power dip hits the basecoat cell — 7 robots throw A1HV/24VoltPower:Val lo within 27 seconds; supervisory alarms trip 12 min later; the booth records "Cell NOT Ready."

Today
The downtime log blames the booth/cell. Maintenance chases robot-by-robot symptoms — the events look unrelated because they're minutes apart and coded differently across systems.
With civops
The cascading-fault view puts the robot sequence-of-events, conveyor state, and downtime entry on one clock. One shared 24V supply dip — not seven faults. Go to the supply, not the manipulators.
10:42

Guided triage at the cell

A unit stops on a likely lens/bracket issue.

Today
The tender hunts across SOPs, prior incidents, parts, and escalation by hand.
With civops

13:00

The 1 PM CCR — a score crosses threshold

The daily Control Center Review. A basecoat robot's predictive score has dropped past threshold.

Today
A spreadsheet recap of yesterday; predictive signals live only in Senseye, untied to cost or age.
With civops
The Asset Health Index shows Robot BC5605 in ALERT — condition slipping, 92% of life consumed, 77% of replacement value already spent on repairs. The verdict isn't "repair again" — it's a planned-replacement call, evidence on one screen.
15:30

Reliability — the pattern, not the incident

Reliability reviews bad actors and repeat failures.

Today
Repeat-failure detection is manual; chronic losses hide inside the "Manufacturing / Starved" buckets.
With civops
The Top-5 Pareto (count and time) and the cross-asset health view surface the chronic themes — and the cleaner event data becomes the training set for the next predictive use case.
End

End of shift — the compounding effect

Handoff to the next team.

Today
The shift's data scatters back into five systems; the next shift starts blind.
With civops
Every event landed once, in context, on the UNS — feeding the cockpit, the AHI, the CCR, and (read-only in phase 1) the Humble agent layer. Tomorrow starts with a trend, not a blank page.
